Arabian Camel

Camels have two sets of eyelashes to shield their eyes from blowing sand

  • Lifespan: 40 years
  • Mass : 400 – 600 kg (Adult)
  • Speed: 65 km/h (Maximum, In Short Bursts)
  • Another name: Dromedary

The word camel comes from the Arabic word: jamal (جمل) which literally means beauty. Camel's hump stores up to 80 pounds of fat, which a camel can break down into water and energy when sustenance is not available. These humps give camels their legendary ability to travel up to 100 desert miles without water.
